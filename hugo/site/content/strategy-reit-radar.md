## What this strategy is trying to capture

REIT Radar is the site's first asset-class-specific screen. It scores only
Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) — companies that own income-producing
real estate and are required to distribute most of their rental income to
shareholders.

> **Only REITs are scored.** Non-REIT stocks do not appear on this page.

This makes it useful for readers who want to compare REITs against each other
on the metrics that matter most for real estate investing.

## Filter criteria

```
industry LIKE 'REIT%'
```

All tickers classified as REITs by Yahoo Finance are included, covering
diversified, industrial, retail, residential, office, healthcare, hotel, 
specialty, and mortgage REITs.

## Why these metrics matter

The score combines three dimensions:

- **Distribution Yield (40% weight)** — The primary reason investors buy
  REITs is income. Higher yield = higher score.
- **Price-to-Book / NAV (30% weight)** — A REIT trading below book value
  (P/B < 1) may be undervalued relative to its real estate assets. Lower
  P/B = higher score.
- **Debt-to-Equity / Leverage (30% weight)** — REITs use debt to buy
  property. Less debt = less risk. Lower leverage = higher score.

## What this page is good for

- Comparing REITs by yield, valuation, and leverage in one view
- Finding REITs that combine strong income with conservative balance sheets
- Building a REIT watchlist for deeper fundamental research

## What it misses

- Occupancy rates and lease expiry profiles
- Property portfolio quality and geographic concentration
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Distribution sustainability (payout ratio)
- Differences between equity REITs and mortgage REITs

A high score means the numbers look attractive, not that the REIT is safe.

## How to use it responsibly

Start with the table below, then open a few ticker pages and ask:

1. Is the yield high because of strong fundamentals or a falling share price?
2. Is the leverage manageable in a rising interest rate environment?
3. What type of properties does the REIT own, and are they in demand?
