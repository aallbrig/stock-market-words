## What this strategy is trying to capture

Dividend Daddy is the site's income-oriented screen. It looks for stocks that pay dividends and then scores them more favorably when they also show relatively lower volatility.

> **Last updated:** March 13, 2026

This makes it useful for readers who want to quickly narrow toward the part of the market that looks more income-focused than speculative.

## Filter criteria

```
dividend_yield > 0 AND has_ma200_data
```

## Why these metrics matter

- **Dividend yield** helps surface companies that return cash to shareholders.
- **Beta** helps distinguish steadier names from more volatile ones.
- **Trading history / MA200 availability** helps avoid extremely sparse or recently listed names.

## What this page is good for

- finding dividend-paying candidates quickly
- comparing yield against volatility
- building a watchlist for more detailed fundamental research

## What it misses

- dividend safety
- payout ratio quality
- balance-sheet stress
- whether the dividend is at risk of being cut

A very high yield can be a warning sign rather than a benefit.

## How to use it responsibly

Start with the table below, then open a few ticker pages and ask:

1. Is the yield unusually high for a good reason or a bad one?
2. Is the business stable enough to support the payout?
3. Does the stock still look reasonable on valuation and trend?
